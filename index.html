thoughts

The new school term is approaching, and my emotions are overwhelmingly tangled. I am about to return to that place I detest, to spend the next few months in tedious days. Study comes first; with the experience gained through stumbling and striving throughout the entire first year of high school, I believe I can improve my current learning state. Yet the only question that irritates me deeply right now is, “Why do I study?”

Perhaps it is to make the road ahead a bit smoother, I sometimes think. But does getting into a good university and landing a good job mean I will never have to endure hardships for the rest of my life? Fantasizing about earning a fortune and living a life of plenty in the future—maybe that is my parents’ greatest expectation for me. Or is it for my own ideal career? That seems somewhat absurd. I have met quite a few people who spout red slogans, and when things happen, they set matters like romance against national development, claiming, “How can we talk about love when our country is not yet prosperous and strong?” As if talking about love is something shameful and degrading. They package themselves as having the iron will of Pavel Korchagin, yet in reality, they can’t move their feet when encountering good-looking people. It’s truly laughable—maybe that’s how they strive for their ideals and beliefs.

In my view, studying now is nothing more than meeting the expectations of all sorts of people in society. A good university and a good job are just baits set for you. When you are about to give up, the school demands a high college graduation rate; your parents hope you can avoid hardships and live that illusory “happy life”; society waves at you, expecting you to be a good person who “touches the society.” But only oneself can know the agony beneath it all. I just don’t understand what that happy life is really like, or how great its allure is. You might laugh at me for not having endured hardships, but please think: what difference is there between that kind of life and Peach Blossom Spring written by Tao Yuanming? Both are fictional. No one’s life is plain sailing. I only hope to seize the happiness within my reach right now, to push me through my high school studies.
